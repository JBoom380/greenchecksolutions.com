Instructions

How to Use This Tracker

Find your jurisdiction in the table below and review the key compliance deadlines, building size threshold, and current enforcement status. Color-coded rows distinguish jurisdictions with active performance enforcement (including financial penalties) from those currently limited to annual benchmarking and disclosure requirements.

If your building is in an Active jurisdiction, you have a legally enforceable deadline and face financial penalties for non-compliance. If you are in a Benchmarking Only jurisdiction, annual energy reporting is required but no performance targets or penalties currently apply — though most of these programs are expected to add performance requirements in the coming years.

Upcoming Jurisdictions to Watch

Several states and cities have pending BPS legislation or are in active rulemaking. Building owners in these areas should begin benchmarking now.

New Jersey
Legislation Pending

Statewide BPS legislation introduced. Covers commercial and multifamily buildings above 25,000 SF. Expected to align with NYC LL97 frameworks.

Massachusetts (statewide)
Rulemaking Active

MEPA regulations and the BERDO model are being considered for statewide adoption. Boston BERDO is already active; broader state legislation advancing.

Virginia
Monitoring

Clean Economy Act sets GHG reduction goals. Commercial building performance standards are under discussion as part of APCO and Dominion rate proceedings.

Maryland
Legislation Pending

Building Energy Performance Standards bill introduced in the General Assembly. Would cover buildings over 35,000 SF with staged compliance deadlines beginning 2028.

Why start benchmarking early? Once performance standards are enacted, you typically have 12–24 months to demonstrate compliance. Buildings already enrolled in ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager with historical data have a significant head start over those scrambling at the last minute.
Status Definitions

What “Active” Means vs “Benchmarking Only”

These two categories represent very different levels of regulatory risk for building owners and operators.

Active Enforcement

Performance Standard in Effect

Your building must meet a specific energy or GHG intensity target by a defined deadline. Failure to comply results in financial penalties that continue until the building achieves compliance.

  • Mandatory EUI or GHG intensity target per building type
  • Annual compliance reporting required
  • Financial penalties for non-compliance (per-SF or per-ton)
  • Non-compliance may affect permits and property transactions
  • Qualified professional involvement typically required
Benchmarking Only

Disclosure Requirement in Effect

Your building is required to measure and report energy use annually, but there is no mandatory performance target yet. Penalties apply only to non-reporting, not to energy performance.

  • Annual energy benchmarking via ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager
  • Data submitted to the relevant city or state agency
  • No EUI or GHG reduction requirement currently
  • Penalties for failing to report (not for energy performance)
  • Most programs expected to add performance requirements in future cycles
The key distinction: In an Active jurisdiction, your energy performance must meet a specific threshold or you pay ongoing financial penalties. In a Benchmarking Only jurisdiction, you must measure and disclose your energy use annually, but there is no consequence for having high energy consumption — yet. Most benchmarking-only programs are on a trajectory toward full performance standards within 3–7 years.
